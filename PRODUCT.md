# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Senior enterprise decision-makers: CIOs, CFOs, and CAIOs evaluating AI and financial intelligence capabilities. They skim dozens of vendor sites and candidate portfolios per week. They are data-literate, pattern-recognizing, and deeply skeptical of noise. They stop for substance.

Context: a brief, deliberate visit. They arrive from LinkedIn, a referral, or a cold pitch. They are assessing whether this person thinks at their level before they ever schedule a call.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio demonstrating mastery of financial intelligence systems — live Python-backed dashboards (Command Center, Decision Impact, Variance Engine) and an AI Business Value Model — to enterprise buyers who need to see evidence, not promises.

Success: the visitor finishes one of the live demos, understands what it does for their business, and reaches out. The portfolio does not try to impress everyone; it earns the trust of the right few.

## Brand Personality

Systematic. Authoritative. Clean.

Voice: confident without overselling. Precise without being cold. The work speaks; the copy doesn't have to shout.

Emotional goal: "This is different from everything else I've seen." Not because it does tricks, but because it is distinctly positioned — the interface rewards people who slow down and look, and they remember it after the tab closes.

## References

Bloomberg Terminal / Refinitiv: data-dense, no-nonsense, built for people who already know what they're looking at. Information as authority. Every element earns its place because the user came to work, not to be sold to.

What's right about it: the density, the purposefulness, the implicit message that the builder respects the reader's intelligence.

## Anti-references

Over-designed agency site: excessive motion, scroll-jacking, cursor effects, WebGL backgrounds, style deployed in place of substance. Prioritizes a designer's portfolio moment over the viewer's time. Collapses under scrutiny.

Also avoid:
- Generic AI portfolio: purple gradients, hero metrics with big numbers, Inter everywhere, every section an identical card grid
- Startup SaaS landing: warm neutral backgrounds, pastel feature grids, copy that explains too much

## Design Principles

1. **Information is authority.** The dashboards and data are the argument. Decoration that competes with the content undermines it.
2. **Built for readers, not viewers.** The interface rewards people who slow down. Depth over spectacle — if it looks the same after 10 seconds as it did in the first glance, it hasn't done its job.
3. **Restraint as craft.** Every animation, shadow, and spacing decision is load-bearing. Nothing exists for atmosphere alone. Motion serves meaning.
4. **Distinctly positioned.** Every visual choice should be deliberate enough that a pattern-recognizing CFO feels the difference — not named differences, felt ones.
5. **Financial precision.** The visual system should feel like it was built by someone who understands how executives read numbers. Tabular figures, accounting notation, consistent hierarchy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum across all surfaces. Active contrast compliance work ongoing (branch: fix/wcag-aa-contrast). Reduced-motion alternatives required for all animations. Semantic HTML and keyboard navigability for all interactive components.
